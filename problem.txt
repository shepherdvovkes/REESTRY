**Key Open Databases and Registries in Ukraine**

Research indicates Ukraine hosts over 80,000 open datasets through its national portal, with numerous state registries promoting transparency. Evidence suggests these cover legislation, judiciary, business, property, and more, though access may vary due to security or updates. While a truly exhaustive list evolves, key ones include government-operated systems, with international complements like HUDOC and WIPO for broader context.

**Core Legislative and Judicial Resources**
- The Verkhovna Rada portal offers laws and amendments, essential for legal analysis (https://zakon.rada.gov.ua).
- The Unified State Register of Court Decisions provides case-law (https://reyestr.court.gov.ua).

**Business and Property Registries**
- The Unified State Register (ЄДР) details entities and beneficiaries (https://usr.minjust.gov.ua).
- Property rights and land cadastre support disputes (https://kap.minjust.gov.ua, https://map.land.gov.ua).

**Other Essential Databases**
- Enforcement and debtors for risk checks (https://asvpweb.minjust.gov.ua, https://erb.minjust.gov.ua).
- Transport, IP, taxes, anti-corruption, and sanctions via respective agencies.
- International: ECHR HUDOC (https://hudoc.echr.coe.int) and WIPO (https://www.wipo.int).

---

Ukraine maintains a robust ecosystem of open databases and state registries, primarily aggregated through the national open data portal at data.gov.ua, which hosts over 80,000 datasets as of late 2025. This framework supports transparency, anti-corruption, and public access, with many registries managed by ministries like Justice, Digital Transformation, and others. While not all datasets qualify as full "databases," the focus here is on publicly accessible registries similar to the user's examples, including legislative, judicial, business, property, and specialized ones. Access may require verification, and some operate in test modes due to ongoing security concerns. The following compiles a comprehensive list from official sources, categorized for clarity, with URLs where available.

### Centralized Open Data Hub
The Unified State Web Portal of Open Data (https://data.gov.ua) serves as the primary aggregator, organizing datasets into categories like State, Economy & Business, Justice & Judiciary, Finance, Health, and Environment. It includes subsets like court decisions for 2018–2021, debtors, financial reporting, and licenses.

Evidence suggests high value for legal and business due diligence, but users should check for updates as some systems transition (e.g., bankruptcy registries).

**Core Legislation and Judicial Resources**
The Verkhovna Rada's portal provides laws, codes, and amendments, essential for legal referencing. For court practices, the Unified State Register of Court Decisions offers case-law for analysis.

**Property and Assets**
Real estate rights and land cadastre databases support property disputes, with public maps for land details.
